For twenty years, "being found online" meant one thing: ranking on Google. That's
still true — but it's no longer the whole picture. A growing share of people now
ask ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity instead of typing a query into a search box,
and those tools don't hand back ten blue links. They give an answer, and they
name specific businesses inside it.

That shift has created a second discipline sitting alongside SEO:
**Generative Engine Optimisation**, or GEO. Here's how the two compare.

## What SEO does

Search Engine Optimisation is the work of getting your pages to rank in
traditional search results. It's well understood and it isn't going anywhere.
Broadly, it comes down to three things:

- **Technical health** — a fast, crawlable, well-structured site.
- **Content** — pages that genuinely answer what people are searching for.
- **Authority** — links and signals that tell Google you can be trusted.

Get those right and you climb the rankings for the terms your customers search.

## What GEO does

Generative Engine Optimisation is the work of getting your business understood,
trusted and **cited inside AI-generated answers**. When someone asks an assistant
"who's a good web design agency in Yorkshire?", it returns a short list of names.
GEO is how you make sure yours is on it.

The signals are related to SEO but not identical. AI models care about:

- **Quotable, structured content** they can extract and reuse cleanly.
- **Consistency** — the same facts about your business appearing across the web.
- **Entity and authority signals** that tell a model who you are and why you're credible.

## The key difference

| | SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Where you appear** | Google's results pages | Inside AI answers (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) |
| **What the user sees** | A list of links to choose from | A single answer naming a few businesses |
| **You win by** | Ranking higher than competitors | Being the source the model cites |
| **Maturity** | Established, competitive | Early — the opportunity is now |

The short version: **SEO is about being one of the options. GEO is about being
the answer.**

## Which one does your business need?

Almost certainly both — but the balance depends on you. If your customers still
overwhelmingly find you through Google, SEO is the priority and GEO is the
forward investment. If you're in a category where people increasingly ask AI for
recommendations, getting cited early is a genuine head start that's hard for
competitors to undo later.

What you shouldn't do is treat them as two separate projects. The content,
structure and authority work that lifts your Google rankings is much of what
makes you quotable to an AI model too. Done together, one effort feeds the other.

> GEO doesn't replace SEO. It extends it — and the businesses that establish
> themselves now will be the ones AI keeps recommending.
